Geo-Fencing to Automate

Revolutionizing Resort Cleaning Management Through Location-Based Automation

My Role:
UI/UX Designer
Duration:
7 weeks
Tools:
Figma, Illustrator
Methods:
Domain Research, Ideation, Prototyping

Problem Statement

Resort cleaning management software was failing to meet staff and management expectations due to inefficient user interface and manual staff login/logout processes. The current system created operational inefficiencies, inaccurate time tracking, and poor user experience that hindered effective resort cleaning operations.

About Resort Cleaning Management Software

Maintaining a clean and well-organized resort is crucial for providing guests with a comfortable and enjoyable experience. The resort cleaning management software is a tool that is used to schedule and manage the cleaning staff at a resort, but its current state of user interface and functionality may not be meeting the expectations of the staff and management. With the aim to enhance the usability and effectiveness of the cleaning management software, this case study will take a deep dive into how a user-centered design approach was employed to revamp the software by improving its UI and incorporating geo-fencing for automatic staff login and logout.

Key Pain Points Identified

  • Inaccurate Time Tracking: Manual login/logout processes led to errors and potential time theft
  • Poor User Interface: Outdated design hindered staff productivity and adoption
  • Operational Inefficiency: Staff spent excessive time on administrative tasks rather than cleaning
  • Real-time Tracking Issues: Management lacked visibility into staff location and task status

Design Solution

Proposed Geo-fencing Solution

I researched, analyzed and proposed a comprehensive solution implementing geo-fencing technology to improve staff login and logout accuracy in this resort cleaning management software. The solution would automate the logging process, provide real-time tracking, and improve efficiency and user-friendliness of the software.

Background on Geo-Fencing Technology

  • Geo-fencing is a location-based technology that uses GPS, RFID, or WiFi to create virtual boundaries around a physical location.
  • Virtual boundaries (geo-fences) created around service locations at the resort, system detects when staff enters or exits and automatically logs the time.
  • Eliminates errors and inaccuracies that can occur with manual logging, and also provides real-time tracking of staff location and status.
  • Can be used to automatically register staff login and logout times based on their location

Step 1: Location Identification

Identify service locations at the resort (guest rooms, common areas, pool areas) and map out cleaning zones.

Step 2: Virtual Boundaries

Create virtual boundaries (geo-fences) around these locations using GPS coordinates with appropriate radius for each zone.

Step 3: Mobile Integration

Install app on staff smartphones that can detect when they are inside or outside of the geo-fences with seamless integration.

Step 4: System Communication

Ensure smooth communication with cleaning management software, making it easy to use and understand for all staff members.

Step 5: Training & Implementation

Train staff on how to use the app and ensure smartphones are properly configured to detect location accurately.

UI Revamp Process

I redesigned the existing resort cleaning management software's user interface to make it more visually appealing, user-friendly and efficient by incorporating modifications to the structure and layout, clear typography, visual elements and responsive design. This redesign improved the overall usability and effectiveness of the software.

Key Learnings & Future Metrics

What I Would Measure if Implementing

  • Time Tracking Accuracy: Percentage reduction in manual time entry errors
  • Staff Productivity: Time saved per staff member per day on administrative tasks
  • User Adoption Rate: Percentage of staff actively using the geo-fencing feature
  • System Reliability: Geo-fence detection accuracy and app uptime metrics
  • Operational Efficiency: Reduction in management time spent on schedule reconciliation
  • User Satisfaction: Staff satisfaction scores before and after implementation
  • Cost Savings: Quantified savings from reduced manual processes and improved accuracy

Design Insights Gained

  • User-Centered Approach: Focusing on staff workflow and pain points was crucial for designing effective solutions
  • Technology Integration: Balancing automation with user control ensures better adoption rates
  • Iterative Design: Multiple iterations of UI components led to significantly improved usability
  • Privacy Considerations: Location-based features require careful privacy design and user consent flows
